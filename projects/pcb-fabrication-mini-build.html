PCB Fabrication Mini Build

A focused hardware mini build where I fabricated two small copper-board PCBs from start to finish through trace layout, etching, drilling, soldering, continuity checks, and LED circuit testing.

Project Overview

From Copper Traces to Working LEDs.

This mini build focused on creating two functional printed circuit boards by turning planned electrical connections into physical copper paths. I transferred the layouts onto copper board, fabricated the traces, assembled the components, and tested the circuits until the LEDs powered correctly.

Although the boards were small, the project gave me hands-on experience with the complete fabrication workflow and showed me how closely layout, manufacturing accuracy, soldering quality, and circuit testing depend on one another.

Development Stage 01

Trace Layout

I began by planning the electrical paths for the two LED circuits. The copper traces needed to connect each component clearly and continuously without relying on a breadboard.

This stage helped me understand how component placement, trace direction, spacing, and connection points affect whether a design can be fabricated and assembled successfully.

Development Stage 02

Board Fabrication

After preparing the layouts, I transferred the designs onto copper board, etched away the unused copper, drilled the component holes, and cleaned the boards before assembly.

This transformed the circuit from a planned layout into a physical board whose remaining copper traces formed the actual electrical paths between components.

Development Stage 03

Circuit Testing

Once the boards were fabricated, I soldered the components, checked the connections, and powered the circuits to confirm that the LEDs lit correctly.

Testing showed how a weak solder joint, damaged trace, misaligned hole, or incomplete connection could prevent the circuit from working. I therefore checked the electrical path carefully rather than assuming the board would work after assembly.

Final Takeaway

Final Reflection.

What this mini build taught me.

This mini build helped me understand how a circuit design becomes a physical electrical path. Seeing the LEDs illuminate made the relationship between trace layout, etching, drilling, soldering, and circuit behaviour much more concrete.

The most important lesson was that small hardware details matter. Even a simple PCB only works when the design, fabrication, component placement, soldering, and testing all align. The process made me more methodical about checking each connection instead of assuming the board would work as soon as it was assembled.
