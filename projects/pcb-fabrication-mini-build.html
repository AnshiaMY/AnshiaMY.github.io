What I Built

A Complete Fabrication Process, Kept Small.

I created two small printed circuit boards by converting planned circuit connections into permanent copper traces. The build was compact, but it allowed me to complete the main stages of PCB fabrication and verify the final circuits through powered LED testing.

01

Plan

Prepared the circuit layouts and identified how the copper traces needed to connect each component.

02

Fabricate

Transferred the layouts, etched away unused copper, drilled the component holes, and soldered the circuits.

03

Test

Checked the electrical connections and powered each board to confirm that the LEDs operated correctly.

Key Takeaway

Small Details Determine Whether Hardware Works.

This mini build made the relationship between a circuit layout and its physical electrical path much more tangible. A damaged trace, misaligned hole, poor solder joint, or incomplete connection could prevent the circuit from working, so I learned to inspect and test each stage rather than assuming the board would function immediately after assembly.
