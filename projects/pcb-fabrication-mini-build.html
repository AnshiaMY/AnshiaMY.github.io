Mini Build Overview

From copper traces to working LEDs.

This mini build focused on the complete hands-on PCB fabrication workflow. I designed the electrical paths, transferred the layout onto copper board, etched the traces, drilled the connection points, soldered the components, and tested the circuit until the LEDs powered correctly.

Build Process

A small build with a full fabrication workflow.

Final Reflection

What this mini build taught me.

This build helped me understand how a circuit design becomes a physical electrical path. Seeing the LEDs light up after fabrication made the connection between trace layout, soldering, continuity, and circuit behaviour much more concrete.

The biggest lesson was that small hardware details matter. A weak solder joint, unclear trace, misaligned drilled hole, or broken connection can stop the circuit from working, so testing each path carefully became just as important as the original layout.
