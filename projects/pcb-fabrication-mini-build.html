What I Built

A Complete Fabrication Process, Kept Small.

I fabricated two copper-clad printed circuit boards by transferring circuit layouts into permanent conductive traces. The process included trace preparation, chemical etching, drilling, component installation, through-hole soldering, continuity checks, and powered functional testing to verify correct LED operation.

01

Prepare

Prepared the supplied circuit layouts for transfer and reviewed the required electrical connections and component positions before beginning fabrication.

02

Fabricate

Transferred the layouts onto copper-clad board, chemically removed the excess copper, drilled the component holes, installed the components, and completed the assemblies using through-hole soldering.

03

Test

Inspected the traces and solder joints, performed continuity checks to identify incomplete or unintended connections, and powered each board to confirm correct LED functionality.

Key Takeaway

Small Details Determine Whether Hardware Works.

This mini build strengthened my understanding of how fabrication quality and assembly accuracy influence circuit performance. Damaged traces, misaligned holes, weak solder joints, or incomplete electrical connections can prevent a board from functioning correctly.

I learned to inspect each stage carefully rather than waiting until the final power test to identify problems. Continuity testing and visual inspection provided evidence that the circuit paths and solder joints were complete before power was applied.
