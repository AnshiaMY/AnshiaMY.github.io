Mini Build Overview

From copper traces to working LEDs.

This mini build focused on creating two small functional printed circuit boards from start to finish. I planned the electrical paths, fabricated the copper traces, soldered the components, and tested the circuits until the LEDs powered correctly.

Build Process

A small build with a full fabrication workflow.

Although the boards were small, the process still followed the core steps of PCB fabrication: layout planning, copper-board production, component assembly, and circuit verification.

01

Trace Layout

Planned the copper paths so current could flow between the components without relying on a breadboard.

02

Board Fabrication

Transferred the design, etched the copper, drilled the connection points, and prepared the boards for soldering.

03

Circuit Testing

Soldered the components, checked the connections, and verified the circuits by powering the LEDs.

Final Reflection

What this mini build taught me.

This mini build helped me understand how a circuit design becomes a physical electrical path. Seeing the LEDs light up made the connection between trace layout, etching, soldering, and circuit behaviour much more concrete.

The biggest lesson was that small hardware details matter. A weak solder joint, unclear trace, misaligned drilled hole, or broken connection can stop the circuit from working, so testing each path carefully became just as important as the original layout.
