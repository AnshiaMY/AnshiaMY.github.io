Project Overview

From Copper Traces To Working LEDs.

This mini build focused on creating two small functional printed circuit boards from start to finish. I planned the electrical paths, fabricated the copper traces, soldered the components, and tested the circuits until the LEDs powered correctly.

Although this was a smaller project, it gave me hands-on experience with the full PCB fabrication workflow and helped me better understand how circuit layout decisions become physical electrical paths.

Build Process

A Small Build With A Full Fabrication Workflow.

The boards were small, but the process still required layout planning, copper-board fabrication, component assembly, and circuit verification.

Development Stage 01

Trace Layout

I started by planning the electrical paths for the two small PCB circuits. The goal was to route the copper traces so current could flow between the components cleanly without relying on a breadboard.

This stage helped me understand how component placement, trace direction, and connection points affect whether a circuit can be fabricated and tested successfully.

Development Stage 02

Board Fabrication

After the layout was prepared, I transferred the design onto copper board, etched away the unused copper, drilled the connection points, and cleaned the boards for assembly.

This step made the process feel much more real because the circuit was no longer just a drawing. The copper traces became the actual electrical paths that the components would depend on.

Development Stage 03

Circuit Testing

Once the boards were fabricated, I soldered the components, checked the connections, and powered the circuits to confirm that the LEDs lit up correctly.

Testing showed how small hardware details can affect the final result. A weak solder joint, unclear trace, misaligned drilled hole, or broken connection could stop the circuit from working, so I had to verify the signal path carefully.

Final Takeaway

Final Reflection.

What this mini build taught me.

This mini build helped me understand how a circuit design becomes a physical electrical path. Seeing the LEDs light up made the connection between trace layout, etching, soldering, and circuit behaviour much more concrete.

The biggest lesson was that small hardware details matter. Even on a small PCB, the circuit only works if the design, fabrication, soldering, and testing all line up. This made me more careful about checking each connection instead of assuming the board would work once it was assembled.
