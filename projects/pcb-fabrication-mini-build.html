What I Built

A Complete Fabrication Process, Kept Small.

I fabricated two copper-clad printed circuit boards by translating circuit layouts into permanent conductive traces. The process included trace preparation, chemical etching, drilling, component installation, through-hole soldering, continuity checks, and powered functional testing to verify correct LED operation.

01

Plan

Prepared the circuit layouts, mapped the required electrical connections, and arranged the copper traces to support accurate component placement and reliable current flow.

02

Fabricate

Transferred the layouts onto copper-clad board, chemically removed the excess copper, drilled the component holes, installed the components, and completed the assemblies using through-hole soldering.

03

Test

Inspected the traces and solder joints, performed continuity checks to identify incomplete or unintended connections, and powered each board to confirm correct LED functionality.

Key Takeaway

Small Details Determine Whether Hardware Works.

This mini build strengthened my understanding of how PCB layout, fabrication quality, and assembly accuracy influence circuit performance. Issues such as damaged traces, misaligned holes, weak solder joints, or incomplete electrical connections can prevent a board from functioning correctly, so I learned to inspect each stage carefully and validate the finished circuit through continuity checks and powered testing.
